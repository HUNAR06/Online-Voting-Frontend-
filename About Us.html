About the Election Commission of India

The Election Commission of India is an autonomous constitutional authority responsible for administering election processes in India. The body administers elections to the Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, State Legislative Assemblies, and the offices of the President and Vice President in the country.

Our Mission & Values

We are committed to maintaining the integrity of the electoral process in India.

Fairness

We ensure that elections are conducted with complete fairness and impartiality, providing equal opportunity to all political parties and candidates.

Integrity

We maintain the highest standards of integrity in the electoral process, ensuring transparency and accountability at every stage.

Inclusivity

We strive to make elections accessible to all citizens, removing barriers that may prevent certain sections of society from participating.

Our History

Tracing the journey of the Election Commission of India through significant milestones.

1950

Establishment

The Election Commission of India was established on January 25, 1950, a day before India became a republic.

1951

First General Election

The first general elections were conducted between October 1951 and February 1952, marking the beginning of democratic elections in India.

1989

Multi-member Commission

The Election Commission became a multi-member body with the appointment of two additional Election Commissioners.

1998

Electronic Voting Machines

Introduction of Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) in state elections, later expanded to national elections.

2022

E-Voting Platform

Launch of the E-Voting Platform to facilitate remote voting and increase accessibility.
