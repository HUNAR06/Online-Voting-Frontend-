Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the electoral process.

How can I check my voter registration status?

You can check your voter registration status on the official Election Commission website by entering your EPIC (Voter ID) number, or by searching with your name, age, gender, and district details. Alternatively, you can call the voter helpline at 1800-11-1950.

What documents are required for voter registration?

For voter registration, you need to submit proof of identity (Aadhaar Card, Passport, Driving License, etc.) and proof of residence (Utility bills, Bank passbook, etc.). Additionally, a passport-sized photograph is required. All documents should be self-attested.

How can I update my address in the voter list?

To update your address, you need to fill Form 8 and submit it along with proof of your new residence. This can be done online through the National Voter Service Portal (NVSP) or by visiting your nearest Voter Service Center.

What is NOTA (None of the Above) option?

NOTA (None of the Above) is an option that allows voters to indicate their disapproval of all the candidates in an election. It's a way to express that none of the candidates deserve your vote. NOTA votes are counted but do not impact the election result.

Can I vote online in Indian elections?

Currently, online voting is not available for general elections in India. Voters need to be physically present at their designated polling station to cast their vote using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs). However, the Election Commission is exploring remote voting technologies for the future.
